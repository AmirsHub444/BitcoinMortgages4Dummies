The Big Idea

Think about how most families ever afford a house. Almost nobody saves up the full price in cash. Instead they use a mortgage: put a little money down, move in today, and pay the rest off month by month for years. At the end, the house is fully theirs.

A Bitcoin mortgage is the exact same move — but the thing you're buying isn't a house. It's Bitcoin.

You put down a small deposit. A computer program (called a smart contract — more on that in a second) buys the Bitcoin and holds onto it for you, like a safe with your name on it. You make fixed monthly payments. When you've paid it off, the safe opens and the Bitcoin is delivered to your wallet — 100% yours.

The Headline Numbers

The deposit can be as small as 2%. That means $162 down gets you a mortgage on $8,100 worth of Bitcoin — at today's price, locked in for the whole loan. You don't need to be rich to start. You need steady income and the discipline to pay every month.

How It Works

The Whole Thing in 5 Steps

1

You Want Bitcoin, But $8,100 Is a Lot of Cash

Saving up the full amount could take years — and the price might run away from you while you save. Sound familiar? It's the same trap as saving for a house in a rising market.

2

So You Put $162 Down Instead

You connect a crypto wallet (that's the whole "application" — no credit check, no paperwork, no banker deciding if you qualify) and deposit your down payment. That $162 opens a mortgage on the full $8,100 of Bitcoin.

3

A Smart Contract Holds the Bitcoin

A smart contract is a program on the blockchain that follows its rules automatically — nobody can change them, cheat them, or make exceptions. It buys the Bitcoin and locks it in custody. Think of it as a robot escrow agent that never sleeps and never takes a bribe.

4

You Pay Monthly — And Here's the Magic

Your price is locked at today's level for the entire loan. If Bitcoin doubles next year, you're still paying it off at the old price — all that gain is yours. And if the price crashes? Nothing happens to your loan. No panic-selling, no getting kicked out of your position. The only rule: keep making your payments.

5

Final Payment → The Safe Opens

The moment the loan is fully paid — whether that took the full term or you paid it off early — the smart contract releases the Bitcoin straight to your wallet. Every sat, yours, forever. No one to ask. It just happens, because the rules say so.

The One Rule

The Timer: The Only Way to Lose

Here's the deal in plain terms. A Bitcoin crash cannot take your position away. There's no "liquidation," no margin call, no forced sale — words you may have heard from other crypto platforms. The market can do whatever it wants.

But there is one rule, and it has teeth: every payment restarts a countdown clock. If you go 45 days without paying, your loan can be defaulted — and when that happens, you lose everything. The down payment, every monthly payment you ever made, and the Bitcoin. Gone. No refund, no partial credit, no do-over.

The Countdown Clock
Real Talk

A house foreclosure at least sells the house and gives you back your equity. This machine gives back nothing. That's the trade: it forgives every market crash and forgives zero missed deadlines. So the golden rule is simple — never open a payment bigger than your worst month can handle, and never, ever ride the clock. Keep 2–3 months of payments saved up before you start. Discipline IS the strategy.

Cheat Sheet

Words to Know

Say These Like You've Known Them for Years
MORTGAGE
Own something big now, pay it off slowly. Small deposit, monthly payments, full ownership at the end.
DOWN PAYMENT
Your upfront deposit. Here it can be as small as 2% — $162 on $8,100 of Bitcoin.
SMART CONTRACT
A program on the blockchain that holds the Bitcoin and enforces the rules automatically. Nobody can change or cheat it.
PRICE LOCK
Your buy-in price is frozen the day you open the loan. Future gains above it are all yours.
LIQUIDATION
When other platforms force-sell your position because the price dropped. Doesn't exist here.
DEFAULT
What happens if you stop paying for 45 days: you lose everything in the contract. The one rule with teeth.
ERF
Early Repayment Fee — a flat 1% cost to pay the whole loan off early, anytime, and take your Bitcoin out.
STABLECOIN
A crypto dollar (like USDC) that stays at $1. It's what you make your payments with.
Straight Answers

Questions You'd Actually Ask

Do I need good credit or a bank account?
No. There is no credit check, no application, and no banker. A crypto wallet is the entire application. You need stablecoins for the deposit and payments — that's it.
What if Bitcoin crashes 50% next week?
Your loan doesn't notice. Nothing is triggered by price. You keep paying the same monthly amount, you keep your full position, and if you believe in Bitcoin long-term, you just keep going. The crash only becomes your problem if it stops you from making payments.
What if Bitcoin moons — can I cash out early?
Yes. Pay off the remaining balance plus a flat 1% Early Repayment Fee, anytime — even before your first payment is due. The smart contract releases the Bitcoin and returns your deposit, and you can hold it or sell it. Your call.
Can I pay extra or pay early each month?
Yes — payments are flexible. Pay any amount, any time, and every payment resets your 45-day countdown. Paying early in your window is the single smartest habit in this whole system.
What's the catch?
The default rule. Go 45 days without paying and you lose everything you've put in — no refund. This machine is generous about market chaos and merciless about missed payments. It rewards exactly one thing: showing up every month. If that's you, the machine works for you.
Is this only for Bitcoin?
Bitcoin and Ethereum are live today (Bitcoin is the flagship). On the roadmap: tokenized gold, and eventually tokenized real-world assets like real estate and treasuries. The same machine, pointed at more and more of the world's wealth.
